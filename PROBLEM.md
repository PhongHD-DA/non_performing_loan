Business Problem Statement: Credit Risk & Delinquency Management

Business Context

In the modern banking landscape, credit card portfolios represent a high-yield but high-risk asset class. Managing these portfolios requires a delicate balance between aggressive growth and rigorous risk control. As transaction volumes increase, traditional manual underwriting processes become a bottleneck, leading to subjective decision-making, slower Turnaround Times (TAT), and an inability to proactively identify emerging risk patterns.

To maintain a competitive edge and ensure financial stability, banks must transition from reactive collection strategies to proactive, data-driven risk management. This project focuses on leveraging historical credit behavior to predict financial distress and optimize the credit approval lifecycle.

Business Problems

The organization currently lacks a centralized, automated system to quantify credit risk accurately, leading to several critical challenges:

Subjective Underwriting: Credit officers often rely on inconsistent qualitative judgments, leading to a non-uniform risk profile across the portfolio.

Lagging Risk Identification: High-risk behaviors, such as credit limit "maxing out," are often detected only after a default has occurred.

Operational Inefficiency: A high volume of low-risk applications is processed manually, wasting expert resources that should be focused on complex, high-value cases.

Non-Performing Loan (NPL) Volatility: Without precise predictive thresholds, the bank is vulnerable to sudden spikes in delinquency rates during economic shifts.

Inflexible Risk Policies: Existing "hard-coded" rules fail to account for the synergy between different risk factors (e.g., combining high utilization with historical past-due records).

Analytical Objectives

This project aims to build an end-to-end predictive analytics solution to:

Quantify Default Risk: Develop a statistical model to calculate the Probability of Default (PD) for every applicant.

Identify Risk Drivers: Determine which behavioral variables (e.g., utilization, debt-to-income) are the strongest leading indicators of distress.

Establish Data-Driven Thresholds: Replace arbitrary rules with mathematically optimized "Red-line" markers.

Segment the Portfolio: Categorize customers into risk-based tiers to enable differentiated credit policies.

Enable Automation (STP): Provide the logic required for a Straight-Through Processing system to automate approvals and rejections.

Key Business Questions

The analysis focuses on answering the following risk and operational questions:

What are the top three behavioral predictors of a customer defaulting within the next 24 months?

At what exact percentage of credit utilization does the probability of default significantly escalate?

How much does the risk increase when a customer has a history of 30-59 days past due compared to a clean record?

What is the impact of a high Debt-to-Income (DTI) ratio on the likelihood of financial distress?

Can we identify a "Synergy Risk" where two moderate risk factors combine to create an unacceptable default probability?

What is the optimal model threshold to maximize the "Recall" of potential defaulters while minimizing the "False Alarm" rate?

How many man-hours can be saved by automating the approval of the bottom 40% of low-risk applicants?

Is age a statistically significant factor in predicting credit discipline in this specific dataset?

Which customer segments are currently over-leveraged and require immediate limit adjustments?

What is the expected reduction in the NPL ratio if the proposed automated rejection logic is implemented?

Expected Business Impact

The final analytics solution is designed to support:

Portfolio Quality: A measurable reduction in the NPL ratio by filtering out high-risk applicants at the point of entry.

Operational Scalability: Reducing manual underwriting workload by 60%, allowing the bank to scale its credit portfolio without proportional hiring.

Improved Customer Experience: Faster approval times (lower TAT) for low-risk, high-value customers.

Regulatory Compliance: Providing a transparent, interpretable (Logistic Regression-based) model that meets audit requirements for fair lending.

Dynamic Risk Strategy: Empowering management to adjust risk appetites instantly by shifting model thresholds based on market conditions.

By transforming raw credit data into a predictive framework, the bank moves from "guessing" risk to "managing" it scientifically.
